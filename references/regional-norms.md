# Regional Resume Norms — 各地区简历习惯

Different regions have very different resume conventions. **Always ask the target
region** — a resume for the same person applying in China vs. US vs. Germany
should look noticeably different.

## Asia — 亚洲

### 🇨🇳 China (大陆)
- **Photo**: Expected (passport-style, top-right corner)
- **Language**: Chinese (Simplified)
- **Page limit**: 1 page (entry-level), 2 pages OK for senior
- **Must include**: Gender, date of birth, political status (党员/团员, for state-owned)
- **May include**: Ethnicity, marital status, hukou (户籍)
- **Avoid**: Overly casual tone
- **Font**: Songti (宋体) or Heiti (黑体)
- **Notes**: State-owned enterprises expect more personal info than private companies

### 🇭🇰 Hong Kong
- **Photo**: Common but not required
- **Language**: English or Traditional Chinese (繁體), sometimes bilingual
- **Page limit**: 1-2 pages
- **Must include**: None beyond standard
- **Font**: Same as CN (Traditional) or English fonts

### 🇹🇼 Taiwan
- **Photo**: Expected
- **Language**: Traditional Chinese (繁體)
- **Page limit**: 1 page preferred
- **Must include**: Gender, date of birth common
- **Font**: 標楷體 or 新細明體

### 🇯🇵 Japan
- **Photo**: Required (3cm × 4cm, formal, glued to form)
- **Language**: Japanese
- **Page limit**: Standardized JIS format (履歴書, rirekisho)
- **Must include**: Date of birth, gender, address, commute time, dependents
- **May include**: Hobbies/interests (趣味・特技)
- **Notes**: Handwritten traditionally valued; format is highly standardized

### 🇰🇷 South Korea
- **Photo**: Required (formal headshot)
- **Language**: Korean
- **Page limit**: Standardized format (이력서)
- **Must include**: Date of birth, gender, military service status (男)
- **Notes**: Large companies (재벌) have their own application formats

### 🇮🇳 India
- **Photo**: Optional (common in traditional companies)
- **Language**: English
- **Page limit**: 2-3 pages OK
- **Must include**: None strict
- **May include**: Date of birth, father's name (traditional), "declaration" at bottom
- **Notes**: Longer resumes accepted; cover letter less common

### 🇸🇬 Singapore
- **Photo**: Common
- **Language**: English
- **Page limit**: 1-2 pages
- **Must include**: Nationality/PR status (work permit matters)
- **May include**: Date of birth, gender, race (CMIO framework)
- **Notes**: Work authorization status is important context

## North America — 北美

### 🇺🇸 United States
- **Photo**: Never (anti-discrimination law — can get your resume rejected)
- **Language**: English
- **Page limit**: 1 page (strict for entry-level/internship)
- **Must include**: Nothing personal — no age, gender, marital status, nationality
- **Avoid**: Photo, date of birth, gender, nationality, religion
- **Font**: Helvetica, Garamond, Calibri, or similar sans-serif/serif

### 🇨🇦 Canada
- **Photo**: No (similar to US)
- **Language**: English or French (depends on province — Quebec = French)
- **Page limit**: 1-2 pages
- **Must include**: None personal
- **Notes**: Very similar to US; bilingual roles may need bilingual CV

## Europe — 欧洲

### 🇬🇧 United Kingdom
- **Photo**: No (similar to US)
- **Language**: English (British spelling: colour, organise, etc.)
- **Page limit**: 2 pages standard (even for junior)
- **Must include**: None personal
- **May include**: "Personal statement" (2-3 sentence summary at top)
- **Notes**: Called "CV" not "resume"; 2 pages is normal

### 🇩🇪 Germany
- **Photo**: Expected (professional headshot, top-right)
- **Language**: German or English (depends on company)
- **Page limit**: 1-2 pages
- **Must include**: Date of birth, nationality, marital status (traditional)
- **May include**: "Lebenslauf" header
- **Notes**: Very structured, chronological format preferred

### 🇫🇷 France
- **Photo**: Common (but legally optional since 2006)
- **Language**: French
- **Page limit**: 1 page preferred
- **Must include**: Date of birth, nationality common
- **Notes**: Called "CV"; handwritten cover letter sometimes expected

### 🇳🇱 Netherlands
- **Photo**: Optional (becoming less common)
- **Language**: Dutch or English
- **Page limit**: 1-2 pages
- **Must include**: None strict
- **Notes**: Direct, concise style valued; English widely accepted in tech

### 🇪🇺 EU General (not covered above)
- **Photo**: Varies by country (Southern/Eastern = more common)
- **Language**: Local language or English
- **Page limit**: Europass format available (standardized EU CV)
- **Notes**: Europass CV is recognized across EU but not required

## Oceania — 大洋洲

### 🇦🇺 Australia
- **Photo**: No
- **Language**: English (Australian spelling)
- **Page limit**: 2-3 pages
- **Must include**: None personal
- **May include**: "Referees" section (references available upon request)
- **Notes**: Called "CV"; more detail expected than US resume

## Middle East — 中东

### 🇦🇪 UAE / Middle East
- **Photo**: Expected
- **Language**: English (Arabic for government roles)
- **Page limit**: 1-2 pages
- **Must include**: Nationality, visa status
- **May include**: Date of birth, marital status, religion
- **Notes**: Nationality and visa status are critical for hiring

## Latin America — 拉美

### 🇧🇷 Brazil
- **Photo**: Optional (less common now)
- **Language**: Portuguese
- **Page limit**: 1-2 pages
- **Must include**: CPF number common, date of birth
- **May include**: Marital status, nationality
- **Notes**: Called "currículo"; Portuguese language required

## Remote / International — 远程/国际

### 🌍 Remote / International
- **Photo**: No (safest default for global applications)
- **Language**: English
- **Page limit**: 1 page
- **Must include**: Time zone, work authorization / visa status if relevant
- **Notes**: Optimize for ATS (applicant tracking systems); plain format

---

## How to apply regional norms

```
1. Ask: "Where are you applying?" (country + company type)
2. Set photo policy based on region
3. Set personal info inclusion based on region
4. Set page limit based on region
5. Set language and font based on region
6. If user applies to MULTIPLE regions → generate separate versions
   (e.g., CN version with photo for China, US version without photo for US)
```
